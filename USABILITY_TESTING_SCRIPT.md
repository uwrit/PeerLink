# PeerLink Usability Testing Plan

**Date:**
**Facilitator:**
**Notetaker:**

**Total time: ~40 minutes**
- Introduction: 5 min
- Pre-task questions: 5 min
- Tasks: 20 min
- Post-task questions: 10 min

---

## Introduction
We will introduce ourselves and explain that we are testing the tool, not the participant. We will ask them to think out loud as they work through the tasks and let them know we won't be answering questions during the tasks.

---

## Pre-Task Questions
We will ask a few quick questions to understand the participant's background:
- What is their role (student, researcher, administrator)?
- Do they have any experience with grant applications or peer review?
- How comfortable are they with using web-based tools?

---

## Tasks

### Task 1 — Explore the Dashboard
We will ask the participant to look at the dashboard and describe what they think it's showing them. We want to see if they can understand the abstract status overview (how many are matched, unmatched, in progress) without any explanation from us.

**Success:** Participant correctly interprets the stats on the page on their own.

### Task 2 — Submit an Abstract for Reviewer Matching
We will give the participant a scenario where their supervisor asked them to find a specific abstract and submit it for reviewer matching at two institutions. We want to see if they can navigate to the Abstracts page, find the right one, and complete the matching form.

**Success:** Participant submits the form with the correct institutions and reviewer count without help.

### Task 3 — Add an Abstract Manually
We will tell the participant that an applicant emailed their abstract directly and they need to enter it into the system manually. We will give them all the necessary info and ask them to add it.

**Success:** Participant finds the Add Abstract page and submits the form correctly.

---

## Post-Task Questions
After the tasks we will ask open-ended questions to get their honest feedback:
- What was their first impression of the tool?
- Were there any moments where they felt confused or lost?
- Was anything missing that they expected to see?
- What would they change about the tool?
- Was there anything they liked?

---

## Facilitator Notes
- If the participant goes quiet we will prompt them with "what are you thinking right now?"
- If they get stuck for more than a minute we will move on and ask what they were expecting to happen
- One person facilitates, one person takes notes during each session
